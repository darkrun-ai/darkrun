Testability review of `.darkrun/darkrun-sim/units/author-spec.md`, "Completion criteria" section — two of the six criteria do not clear the testability bar as written.

(1) Criterion 2 ("All six required sections present in order"): the paired command `grep -c '^#' .darkrun/darkrun-sim/specify/spec.md` reports >= 6 does not test the claim it's attached to. Verified empirically — a spec.md missing 2 of the 6 required sections (`## Contracts`, `## Edge cases`) but carrying the mandated >=12 `### AC-n` subheadings scores `grep -c '^#'` = 16, which satisfies ">= 6" even though the claim ("all six required sections present") is false. The AC-n subheadings alone (required elsewhere to number >=12) push the raw '#'-line count well past 6 regardless of whether the six named top-level sections exist, so the command can report success while the criterion is false — it is not a valid automated check of this claim. The manual heading-order half of criterion 2 is fine (the "Required document structure" section gives exact heading text for all 6 headings in backticks, so two independent checkers can agree), but the automated command should either be dropped or replaced with one that actually verifies presence of the six named headings (e.g., grep for the 6 literal heading lines, not a raw '#' count).

(2) Criterion 6 ("No hedging or placeholders as criteria or boundaries: no `TBD`, `should`, `probably`, `might`, `etc.`"): the list of banned words ends in "etc." — an open, undefined set, embedded inside a requirement whose entire purpose is banning undefined/open-ended language. This is the exact defect class the testability review exists to catch (an untestable word left undefined, used as a requirement). Two independent checkers could disagree on whether a word outside {TBD, should, probably, might} — "likely," "generally," "typically" — violates the criterion, because "etc." doesn't close the set. Unlike criteria 1, 3, 4, and 5 (each paired with a literal grep/test command), criterion 6 has no literal command at all. Fix: replace "etc." with a closed, enumerated list of banned terms, or state the check as a fully-specified grep pattern, so the criterion has a single yes/no answer without asking the author what "etc." was meant to include.

Everything else clears the bar: criteria 1, 3, 4, 5 are literal, deterministic commands; the three declared quality gates (artifact-nonempty, citations-resolve >=10, acceptance-criteria-floor >=12) all correctly fail (non-zero exit) when dry-run against the current worktree where `.darkrun/darkrun-sim/specify/spec.md` does not yet exist — none passes vacuously. No other hedge/untestable words found used as requirements in the unit body; the operator decisions are locked and not relitigated.
