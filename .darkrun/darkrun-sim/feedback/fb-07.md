Completeness audit of `.darkrun/darkrun-sim/specify/spec.md` (verified matching `darkrun/darkrun-sim/specify` branch): internal contradiction on decision provenance.

**Contract 3** (`## Contracts` → "the darkrun-core fixture schema"), the paragraph introducing the Normalization rules, reads:

> "Normalization rules, applied before serialization (per the frame's "determinism by normalization at projection" decision):"

This attributes "determinism by normalization at projection" to `.darkrun/darkrun-sim/frame/frame.md`.

But the spec's own **Out of scope** section states the opposite attribution:

> "Reopening any of the frame's four locked operator decisions, or this station's three operator decisions (crate partition, fixture-schema placement, determinism-by-normalization)."

Here "determinism-by-normalization" is explicitly listed as one of *this station's* (specify's) own three operator decisions, distinct from and additional to the frame's four locked decisions.

I read `frame.md` in full (84 lines) and grepped it for `normali|determin|nonce|schema_version|timestamp` — zero matches. The frame contains no discussion of fixture normalization, timestamp scrubbing, `verifier_nonce` placeholder substitution, or determinism-by-projection anywhere. Its four locked decisions (dark-mode-first, scripted-provider-only, crate placement, no-wasm-LLM) are unrelated to this topic. So the Out-of-scope line's classification is the accurate one; Contract 3's "per the frame's ... decision" phrase misattributes a station-level decision to the frame.

This is not cosmetic: which tier a decision belongs to determines its reopening process. A frame-locked decision requires reopening the frame (out of this station's authority, several of the frame's four decisions carry explicit re-entry triggers or are declared permanently dead). A station-level operator decision reopens at this station. A builder or a later reviewer reading Contract 3 in isolation would believe the normalization approach is frame-authority-locked and therefore not something even this station could revisit — when the spec's own Out-of-scope section says otherwise.

Requested fix: reword Contract 3's parenthetical to attribute the decision correctly, e.g. "(per this station's own 'determinism by normalization at projection' operator decision — see Out of scope)" instead of "(per the frame's ... decision)". No other change needed; the normalization rules themselves (items 1-3) are well-specified and internally consistent with AC-7, AC-6, and the rest of Contract 3.
