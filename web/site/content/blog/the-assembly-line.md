# The assembly line for software

Nobody strapped a horse to a Model T. When the tool changed, the work changed
with it. Agents are that kind of change, and most teams are still strapping the
horse on — pointing a model at a blank editor and hoping.

darkrun takes the other path. It treats software the way a factory treats a
product: an ordered line of stations, each retiring one class of risk before the
next begins. The model does the work at each station. The line keeps the work
honest.

The win isn't speed for its own sake. It's that a mistake caught in Frame costs
nothing, and the same mistake caught in Harden costs a rewrite. Order the line by
the cost of late discovery, and you pay for every mistake while it is still
cheap.

That is the whole idea. Build the tracks, then let the train run.
