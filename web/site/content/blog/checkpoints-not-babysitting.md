# Checkpoints, not babysitting

The fastest way to waste a human is to make them watch an agent type. Attention
is the scarce resource, and spending it on keystrokes means there's none left for
the decisions that actually matter.

darkrun spends your attention at the checkpoints. Low-risk stations clear on an
**auto** checkpoint and you never see them. The moment a station hits something
that needs judgment — a design trade-off, a release sign-off, a long external
task — it stops on an **ask**, **external**, or **await** checkpoint and pulls
you in.

You stay in the loop where being in the loop changes the outcome, and out of it
everywhere else. That's the difference between driving a line and babysitting one.
